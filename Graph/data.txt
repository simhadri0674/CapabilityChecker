Sir Mo Farah (born Hussein Abdi Kahin) is a retired British long-distance runner and the most successful male track distance runner in history. A four-time Olympic gold medallist and six-time World Champion, he is famous for achieving the "double-double" (winning the 5,000m and 10,000m at consecutive Olympics in 2012 and 2016) and the "quadruple-double" when including his World Championship titles. He officially retired from professional competition in September 2023.

Personal Background & Recent Life
Trafficking Revelation: In July 2022, Farah revealed his birth identity is Hussein Abdi Kahin. He was illegally trafficked from Djibouti to the UK at age nine and forced into domestic servitude. His PE teacher, Alan Watkinson, later helped him gain citizenship under the name Mohamed Farah.
Retirement: His final competitive race was the Great North Run on 10 September 2023.
Current Activities (2026): Farah currently resides with his family in Doha, Qatar, and is involved in sports-tech entrepreneurship and philanthropy.
Honours: He was appointed CBE in 2013 and received a knighthood from Queen Elizabeth II in 2017.
